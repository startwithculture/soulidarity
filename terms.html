1. Applicant(s) and represented musician(s) must be 18 years and older.
2. The applicant must be the band manager or main agent (please email us if you do not have a manager or agent).
3. Application must be made through the Soulidarity online application form.
4. Applicant(s) and any represented musician(s) do NOT have to be sick with Covid-19 to apply for the fund.
5. The applicant can be resident anywhere BUT the beneficiary musician(s) must be resident in Africa. (Musicians living outside of Africa will not be considered)
6. Â¾ (three quarters) of the concerts lost by the participating musician(s) must have been booked outside the African continent
7. Applicant(s) and musician(s) agree to share and promote the campaign within their network and social media throughout the duration of the campaign.
8. Applicant(s) agree to share the SOULIDARITY campaign with all the promoters who had booked the represented musicians in order to encourage the promoters to participate and help promote the campaign.
9. Applicant(s) and the represented musician(s) are required to provide promotional material for the campaign such as pictures, shout outs and videos, More info: www.soulidarity.org/application.html
10. START WITH CULTURE has the right to reject applications that are incomplete, faulty or do not fit the set criteria.
11. Applications can only be made per band/group/artist and not by individual band members.
12. Musicians who play in different bands can only submit and be considered under one band - please choose the band you had the most shows with.
13. Applicant agrees to provide further documentation as proof upon request for example concert contracts, cancelation confirmations, and contact details of the promoter.
14. All information given by the applicant will be treated as 100% confidential and will not be used in any other way other than for the purposes of evaluating submitted applications. Applicant agrees to the START WITH CULTURE data collection and privacy policies. (See our data protection policies here www.soulidarity.org/privacy.html)
15. Participation in the SOULIDARITY campaign does not automatically entitle participants to any right to payments.
16. Applicant has understood that payments can only be made if money has been raised through the SOULIDARITY fundraising effort.
17. The relief payment amount can be adjusted as a percentage in relation to the funds collected.
18. Participant(s) agree not to make any further demands/requests from START WITH CULTURE after receiving the relief fund payment.
19. Participants must provide passport copies of all band members as the only accepted proof of identity.
20. The applicant can only retain no more than 20% of the relief payment and the rest must be forwarded to the represented musician(s). Proof of payment to the beneficiary musicians can be demanded by START WITH CULTURE at any time. In case the applicant fails to provide proof to START WITH CULTURE, START WITH CULTURE can claim back the payment made to the applicant and in such a case, START WITH CULTURE will then pay directly to the beneficiary musicians . Applicants can also give START WITH CULTURE the banking details for individual musicians.
21. Applicant agrees that all banking charges and payment provider fees will be due to the recipient of the money.
22. Before the payment is made, the applicant will receive a document by email, which he/she must sign and send back within 7 days.
23. Applicant has understood and agreed that the fund is NOT a FEE replacement for lost concerts but it is designed to provide relief to musicians in a time of need. The relief payment does not need to be paid back to START WITH CULTURE.
24. Non-discrimination statement: START WITH CULTURE highlights that the fund does not discriminate in any way in its processes and it covers musicians making music in any genre as long as they fit the set criteria.
25. Deadline for the applications is  30th of June 2020 midnight.
